Pros: UNCC has a good stem program, besides physics. The campus is almost completely self contained, meaning that the only people that are around campus are people on official business with the school… at least for the most part. I know that NC State for example has its campus spread across Raleigh, which has to suck for the students. If you want to go to Charlotte downtown or wherever, there’s a light rail on campus and it’s free for students up to a certain point. Never took it myself, so can’t say what point. I really like all of the professors that I’ve taken classes with the last few semesters, I’m in the math dept though, so I can’t really speak to professors of most other colleges. UNCC has 7 colleges, each offering a lot of degrees. No worries, all 7 colleges are on the university campus, and typically you’ll take classes from multiple colleges, but probably not from all of them. UNCC has a lot of dining options. They just built the UREC in 2019. It’s a giant, 5 story gym, with an indoor running track, a massive pool, that is easily the size of 5 pools combined, if not more, indoor basketball courts, I think it has indoor tennis courts, a whole floor dedicated to cardio, weightlifting areas, etc., I think there are other pros that I’m not thinking of, but we’ll leave it there.

Cons: The crime in the area, while campus is generally safe, the area immediately around the campus is kind of sketchy, there isn’t very many food options for those who have food allergies. I know a guy that was managing to go gluten free, I don’t know how he found food to eat. Another con, the physics dept. is absolutely terrible. I’m sure there are also more cons that I’m not thinking of.

Anyway, point is that I really love this school, and wouldn’t go anywhere else. However, it’s not the school for everyone. Tour it before you decide to come here.
