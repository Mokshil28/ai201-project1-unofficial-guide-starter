So I’m a transfer student from NC State originally who just came to Charlotte. I’m a commuter so I can’t really speak for the parties. As for the people thus far, everybody is super kind and friendly. I never had any friends at NC State, but in this first week, I made several connections!

I’ve lived in Charlotte my entire life. There’s always something to do. Just right off campus, there are multiple shopping centers, restaurants, etc. there are things like top golf, concord mills (a nice mall in the area), and obviously the city itself and there are plenty of things to do (museums, concerts, bars, etc). As a UNCC student, you get access to the light rail for free, and UNCC has its own bus that takes students to shopping centers.

Academically, the teachers here are great. My teachers at NC State hardly taught and everything was self taught. My professors at Charlotte (as an engineering major) seem super passionate about what they teach and genuinely care about the success of their students.

From what I’ve seen, the dorms are nice and the dining options on campus are tons better. We have places like subway, Panda Express, bojangles, Wendy’s, chick fil a, and more.

Campus in general is beautiful. I don’t think I’ve ever seen buildings like the ones I have class in! Also since you’re super athletic, I think you would like UREC. It’s an amazing facility with lots of fitness options available. They also have some amazing recreational areas for general sports, so if you play tennis, you get access to the courts as well.

Lastly, there are many on-campus amenities and events. We have a movie theater, a lounge in the student union that has billiards and gaming stations, a botanical garden, a recreational outdoor pool, and more. Some events we do are Gold Rush which is the welcome week event, which has many many MANY opportunities to connect.

I promise you Charlotte was the greatest decision I’ve ever made. I love it here! DM me if you have any questions.

I did 2 years at state in computer science. Worst decision ever. 0 help from professors, impossible to get help in office hours, constantly belittled by them, and found them very anti-military. Had a few friends commit suicide and know some who got cancer from Poe hall. I had the same issues with having to be almost completely self taught.

There’s a million more career fairs and opportunities here compared to state since we’re in Charlotte. I’ve been able to actually get campus jobs and get all the help I need to get a 4.0 gpa consistently. Had to retake some com sci classes here and the content is nearly identical (besides some programming classes). Professors seem to actually care about us in my experience.

I will say, I did have a better time with friends at NCSU and Raleigh is safer + cheaper … but that’s all. There are great departments with very good culture but the com sci department was terrible to me, and often found people leaving com sci for other engineering disciplines.

Everyone has their own unique experience, but I wouldn’t recommend NCSU overall compared to Charlotte.
