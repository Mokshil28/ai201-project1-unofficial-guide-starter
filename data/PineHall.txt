A friend of mine stayed there my sophomore year so I went over there a lot to work on projects,

Compared to Belk or Martin which were the dorms I stayed in 3 of the 4 years, it’s smaller, rooms just aren’t as big or tall. The building layout was a little weird, with a central staircase per hallway with each half story landing having 2 rooms. One of the standout features of the building were most of the rooms having a balcony connected to the common living space which can be nicer in the non summer/mid winter months. I do remember my friend having some AC problems, I forget if the unit broke or if air movement was just slow and it got stuffy.

In terms of location on campus it’s one of the better ones. There’s the student Union on one side, main general educational buildings across the street, and the science building on the other side of which has Auntie Anne’s. Parking, per usual, isn’t the best but there are parking spots right up to the buildings so if you do get a spot there then you don’t have to walk far. Overall I’d say the best thing going for Pine is its close location to the Union, parking if you can get it, and proximity to a lot of the education buildings. But for apartment style dorms I’d say it’s on the lower end in terms of space.
