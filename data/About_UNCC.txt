I feel like this will likely take a negative turn, but I’ll offer something positive.

I always enjoyed how it felt like Charlotte was full of opportunity to anyone who was willing to seize it. Speaking as someone who went to a wealthy, small, elite, private school for grad school, I saw an environment where outcomes were destined from Day One when students stepped foot on campus. Daddy’s golfing buddies had already set up Little Johnny with an elite internship, regardless of how he stacked up against his peers.

Charlotte never felt like that. Charlotte felt like if you came here, worked hard, and did well, the sky was the limit. Conversely, things weren’t just going to be handed to you just because of who you were or where you came from.

In a very naive sense of what the American Dream is, Charlotte felt like it fit that narrative that you can come here and truly make your experience whatever you dream it to be. Nobody told me that was what this place was like, and I’m forever thankful for it.

UNCC has a lot of resources, but doesn't tell you about them. There is a specific major for just about anything you'd want to do with a psychology degree besides strict psychology.
Because many people don't know this, the psychology major is bloated with something like 1600 students.
Those 1600 students make getting help from advisors extremely difficult ( I haven't even spoken to mine in about two years ).
The department has little to no connection to resources outside the campus, and on campus, not every professor has a research lab, and if they do, labs only take a small handful of people at a time. And what do psych grad schools want to see? That you have valid research experience.
Additionally, grad schools want to see that you typically have at least three letters of recommendation. It is extra helpful if some of those letters come from professionals in your field. This is very hard to do when there are some 1600 students who might pass by them and also seek them. Even harder yet, when a significant portion of your teachers teach online classes. Last I heard, the psychology department was even pushing for more online classes.
This is my final semester, and I have only secured some from Psyche just now.
So, very rough being a psychology major. To be fair, even with all this, I still probably would have gone to UNCC due to its modest cost and convenience, but it would have been good to know, and I could have started doing prep-work with all this in mind much sooner.
